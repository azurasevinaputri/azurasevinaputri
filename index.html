1. Black Cat Day

You will rarely find any day dedicated to an animal; however, black cats have two days. “Black Cat Appreciation Day” is celebrated on the 17th of August in the USA, and “National Black Cat Day” is celebrated in the UK on the 27th of October. The tradition in both countries started in 2011 to show love for black cats and create awareness among general people about false beliefs.

2. Sign Of Misfortune Or Fortune?

It’s the most intriguing section of the story. Black cats are considered good luck in some parts of the world and signs of bad luck in some other areas. In some parts of South East Asia, people believe that if a black cat intercepts your way at the start of the journey, postpone your plan, as it is a sign that something bad will happen on the way. On the other hand, in Japan, France, and England, black cats are taken as a sign of good fortune and wealth. Scottish people believe that a black cat entering your door will bring success and money. Japanese sailors also take black cats with them, considering that these cats will protect them from seas storm and other obstacles. Pirates, however, believed that a black cat walking towards you was a sign of misfortune. A cat that walked away from you meant good fortune, and if a black feline entered the ship and then leaped off, the boat was most likely to sink.

3. Have More Resistance Against Diseases

After discussing so many false beliefs and superstitions, here’s the first scientifically approved fact about black cats. The stunning black color makes a cat look good and makes him feel good. Scientists believe that black color cats are more resistant to different kinds of diseases than cats of other colors. The mutation that can cause a cat’s fur color to become black is part of the same genetic lineage as the genes that give humans the ability to resist illnesses like HIV. Studies are in progress to entirely understand the cat’s genome, hoping that it will help cure HIV in humans.

4. Can Help Your Love

In some regions of the world, if a black cat enters your house, you are more likely to meet your loved one or enjoy a good relationship. According to Japanese culture, single girls keep a black cat in the house, hoping to get better proposals. In the Midlands of the United Kingdom, it is common practice to gift a black cat to brides, as it is believed that a black cat will bring happiness and good fortune in the bride’s life.
